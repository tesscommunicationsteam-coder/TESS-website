Message from the President

Why TESS exists

President
Ying-Hwa (Bill) Kuo
Taiwanese-American Earth Science Society

Four decades of U.S.–Taiwan collaboration in earth science

For over forty years, U.S.–Taiwan collaboration in earth system science has advanced both nations and the broader scientific community. From the 1987 Taiwan Area Mesoscale Experiment (TAMEX) — the first major mesoscale field experiment in East Asia — to the 2006 COSMIC satellite mission that delivered the world's first real-time constellation of GNSS radio occultation data, these partnerships have shaped modern weather prediction and climate monitoring.

Taiwanese scientists working in the U.S. have played a unique and critical role, understanding and connecting the science communities on both sides of the Pacific. Yet many work in isolation, with little networking among fellow Taiwanese scientists across the field.

As first-generation immigrants, Taiwanese-American scientists face challenges including language barriers, cultural differences, and communication hurdles that can slow career progression. TESS was created to change that — providing mentorship, connection, and a professional home.

Event Highlight

Celebrating our launch

March 18, 2026 · NSF NCAR Mesa Laboratory

TESS Luncheon — celebrating our establishment & U.S.–Taiwan science collaboration

TESS hosted its first event celebrating its official launch and long-term U.S.–Taiwan collaboration in atmospheric sciences. Around 40 participants attended, including Director General Debby Huang (TECO Denver), Dr. Li-Wei Chou (TECO Los Angeles), and senior leaders from UCAR, NCAR, and NOAA.

Dr. Chou introduced funding programs for U.S.–Taiwan collaboration, while the TESS president presented the society's background, mission, and logo (designed by board member Chih-Ting Hsu).
